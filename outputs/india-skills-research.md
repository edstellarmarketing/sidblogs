## VISA / IMMIGRATION PATHWAYS

### Employment Visa (E-Visa)
- **Purpose:** For foreign nationals taking up skilled employment in India
- **Minimum Salary:** ₹16.25 LPA (~USD 25,000) annually
- **Exemptions:** Foreign specialist chefs/ethnic cooks, language teachers (other than English), embassy staff
- **Duration:** Initially 1 year, renewable annually up to 5 years maximum
- **Requirements:** Formal employment contract with an Indian-registered entity, proof of specialized skills/qualifications, employer sponsorship
- **Processing Time:** 10-20 working days, varying by embassy and nationality
- **Post-Arrival:** Registration with FRRO (Foreigners Regional Registration Office) within 14 days if stay exceeds 180 days

### Business Visa
- **Purpose:** For attending business meetings, establishing business contacts, exploring business opportunities
- **Duration:** Up to 5 years (multiple entry)
- **Restriction:** Cannot take up employment on a business visa

## FAQ DATA

### Q1: What are the most in-demand skills in India for 2026?
The most in-demand skills in India for 2026 include AI/Machine Learning, Generative AI, Data Science, Cybersecurity, Cloud Computing/DevOps, and Full-Stack Development. According to the ManpowerGroup 2026 Global Talent Shortage Survey, AI Model & Application Development (39%) and AI Literacy (38%) are the two hardest skills for employers to find. The India Skills Report 2026 by Wheebox confirms that Technology, BFSI, Manufacturing, Renewable Energy, and Healthcare are the top hiring sectors.

### Q2: What is the average salary for AI and Machine Learning engineers in India?
AI and ML engineers in India earn between ₹6 LPA for freshers and ₹60 LPA+ for senior professionals. The average AI/ML Engineer salary is approximately ₹15-20 LPA for mid-level roles (3-5 years experience). Bangalore offers the highest compensation at ₹18-35 LPA for mid-level positions, followed by Hyderabad at ₹15-28 LPA. Workers with AI expertise earn 56% more than their peers without these skills (Taggd IT Hiring Trends 2026).

### Q3: How large is India's skills gap in 2026?
India faces a significant and growing skills gap. According to NASSCOM and the World Economic Forum, India faces a potential workforce skill deficit of 47-49 million workers by 2027. The ManpowerGroup 2026 survey shows 82% of Indian employers report difficulty finding skilled talent, up from 80% in 2025. Specific shortages are acute in AI/ML (only 16% of IT professionals are AI-skilled), cybersecurity (shortage of nearly 1 million professionals), and data roles (60-73% demand-supply gap per NASSCOM).

### Q4: Can foreign professionals work in India's tech sector?
Yes, foreign professionals can work in India through the Employment Visa (E-Visa). The primary requirement is a minimum annual salary of approximately ₹16.25 LPA (~USD 25,000). The visa is initially granted for one year and can be renewed annually up to 5 years. The best cities for foreign tech workers are Bangalore, Hyderabad, Mumbai, Delhi NCR, and Pune. Registration with FRRO within 14 days of arrival is mandatory for stays exceeding 180 days.

### Q5: Which Indian cities offer the highest tech salaries?
Bangalore consistently offers the highest tech salaries in India, 15-25% above the national average. Hyderabad ranks second and provides the best salary-to-cost-of-living ratio, with salaries only 5-10% below Bangalore but rent 30-40% lower. Mumbai offers competitive salaries especially in BFSI and fintech but has the highest cost of living. Delhi NCR is strong for corporate and consulting roles. Pune and Chennai are growing hubs with lower living costs (ResumeGyani, Scaler 2026).

### Q6: What certifications boost salary the most for Indian tech professionals?
Certifications can increase salaries by 10-25% across tech roles. For Cloud/DevOps, AWS Solutions Architect and Kubernetes certifications provide the highest premiums. In Cybersecurity, CISSP and CEH are most valued. PMP-certified professionals who understand Scrum earn 30% more than pure Scrum Masters (ShriLearning). For AI/ML, Google and AWS machine learning certifications command premium compensation. Green energy LEED-certified professionals earn 20-40% more (Sustainability17).

### Q7: Is the gig economy a viable career path in India?
India's gig economy is expanding rapidly. The gig workforce has grown from 7.7 million in FY2021 to 12 million in FY2025, projected to reach 23.5 million by 2030 (India Skills Report 2026). India supplies 24% of global freelance talent with 15 million active freelancers. Non-agricultural gigs are projected to contribute ₹2.35 lakh crore to GDP by 2029-30. Growth is driven by 800+ million smartphone users and 15 billion monthly UPI transactions.

### Q8: How is India preparing to close the digital skills gap?
India has launched multiple initiatives including Future Skills Prime (MeitY-NASSCOM joint initiative to reskill 2 million professionals), PMKVY (14 million+ beneficiaries enrolled), and NASSCOM's new Talent Council led by Infosys. ISACA and NASSCOM have partnered to standardize digital skills. Overall employability has risen from 46.2% in 2022 to 56.35% in 2026 (India Skills Report 2026), with female employability (54%) now surpassing male employability (51.5%).
