## KEYWORD DENSITY RECOMMENDATIONS

Based on competitor analysis, the SID blog for India should prioritize:

1. **Title tag must include:** "skills in demand in India 2026" (used by 4+ competitors)
2. **H1 should include:** "skills in demand in India" + year
3. **H2 headings should name individual skills:** "Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning," "Data Science and Analytics," "Cybersecurity," "Cloud Computing," etc.
4. **Body must reference:** salary ranges in INR/LPA, NASSCOM data, India Skills Report 2026, ManpowerGroup talent shortage data, city-level demand (Bengaluru, Hyderabad, Delhi/NCR)
5. **FAQ section should target:** "what are the most in-demand skills in India 2026," "which industry is hiring the most in India," "what is the highest paying skill in India"
6. **Avoid keyword stuffing:** Competitors that rank well use each primary keyword 2-4 times naturally, not more

---

## COMPETITOR CONTENT GAPS (OPPORTUNITIES)

The following topics are underrepresented in competitor content and present differentiation opportunities:

1. **Salary explorer by city** - Most competitors give only national averages; a city-by-city salary breakdown (Bengaluru vs Mumbai vs Delhi vs Hyderabad vs Pune vs Chennai) would be unique
2. **Skill-to-industry mapping** - Few pages connect specific skills to specific Indian industries in a structured way
3. **Interactive quiz element** - No competitor page includes a "which skill should you learn" assessment
4. **Visa/global mobility angle** - Almost no competitor covers how in-demand Indian skills translate to global job markets
5. **Government skilling programmes** - Skill India Digital Hub, FutureSkills PRIME, and PMKVY are mentioned only in passing; a detailed guide would add value
6. **Gig economy skills** - The 23.5 million gig worker projection is cited but no competitor builds a dedicated section on freelance/gig-specific skills
7. **Tier-2/Tier-3 city demand** - Only P1 mentions non-metro hiring; this is an underserved angle
