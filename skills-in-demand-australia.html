Why Australia's Skills Market Matters in 2026

Australia has entered what labour market analysts describe as a structural shortage era. Low unemployment, an ageing workforce, record infrastructure investment, and accelerating digital transformation have combined to create a demand environment where nearly three in ten assessed occupations cannot be adequately filled. For individuals, this represents extraordinary opportunity. For organisations, it is an urgent call to invest in workforce capability.

This guide draws on the Jobs and Skills Australia 2025 Occupation Shortage List, LinkedIn's Jobs on the Rise 2026, Hays Salary Guide FY25–26, the Core Skills Occupation List (CSOL), and data from CSIRO, Tech Council of Australia, and the ABS to give you the most current, actionable picture of in-demand skills in Australia.

Skills in Demand Visa & the CSOL — 2025 Update

On 7 December 2024, Australia replaced the Temporary Skill Shortage (TSS/482) visa with the new Skills in Demand (SID) visa. The Core Skills Occupation List (CSOL) — 456 occupations developed by Jobs and Skills Australia — forms the backbone of the Core Skills stream.

Key CSOL / SID Visa Facts

  • The CSOL contains 456 occupations eligible for the Skills in Demand visa Core Skills stream
  • From 1 July 2025, the Core Skills Income Threshold (CSIT) increased to $76,515
  • The Specialist Skills Income Threshold (SSIT) is $141,210 for high-earning specialists
  • The CSOL also applies to the Direct Entry stream of the permanent ENS (subclass 186) visa
  • Regional visa (subclass 491) offers additional migration points for roles in regional Australia
🏛️
Australian Qualifications Framework (AQF)

The AQF is Australia's national policy standardising qualifications across VET and higher education. Skills assessment for CSOL migration purposes typically requires recognition under the AQF (Certificate III–IV for trades; Diploma or Degree for professional roles). Overseas qualifications must be assessed by the relevant Australian authority (e.g., Engineers Australia, AHPRA, AITSL).

Australia Critical Skills Shortage List — 2025

The Jobs and Skills Australia Occupation Shortage List (OSL) is the definitive reference for Australia's skill gaps. In 2025, 29 new occupations were added, while 69 occupations were removed (primarily from technology and finance as pipelines recovered). 139 occupations remain in persistent shortage for five consecutive years.

In-Demand Skills in Australia for Foreigners

For the 2025–26 financial year, Australia allocated 185,000 permanent migration places, with 71% (132,148) reserved for skilled workers. If you are planning to work in Australia from overseas, aligning your skills with the CSOL is the most critical strategic decision you can make.

Skills in Demand (SID) Visa — Core Skills Stream

To apply for the SID visa via the Core Skills stream, you must meet all of the following:

  • Your occupation appears on the Core Skills Occupation List (CSOL) — 456 occupations
  • Positive skills assessment from the relevant Australian assessing body
  • A genuine job offer from an approved Standard Business Sponsor
  • English proficiency: minimum IELTS 6.0 (or equivalent PTE, TOEFL, OET)
  • Earnings meeting or exceeding the Core Skills Income Threshold (CSIT): $76,515/year (from July 2025)
  • Health and character requirements

Fastest Sectors for Sponsorship Approval

  • Healthcare — GPs and nurses have priority processing; AHPRA registration required; strong regional demand
  • Skilled Trades — Electricians and plumbers are consistently sponsored; trade licences must be assessed state-by-state
  • Education — AITSL assessment required; regional pathways (491) offer fastest entry for teachers
  • Technology — Software engineers, AI engineers, and cybersecurity specialists are sponsored across all states
  • Engineering — Civil and structural engineers needed for infrastructure pipeline; Engineers Australia assessment required
  • Aged Care — Lowest barriers to sponsorship; Certificate III qualification often sufficient for initial entry

Best Cities by Skill Sector

  • Technology (AI, Cybersecurity, Software): Sydney (+10–15% salary premium) and Melbourne — both have major tech hubs and international employer bases
  • Mining (Electricians, Engineers): Perth and Darwin — FIFO roles available; WA mining pays significant premiums
  • Healthcare: Regional Australia — bonus migration points via 491 visa; highest vacancy rates and fastest approvals outside metro areas
  • Finance: Sydney CBD, Melbourne Docklands — ASX and major bank headquarters
  • Education: All states — regional schools have the most acute need and strongest visa pathway support
  • Construction: Brisbane, Melbourne — major infrastructure pipeline through 2030

Typical Migration Timeline

Skills Assessment

4–12 weeks depending on the assessing body. Engineers Australia, AHPRA, and AITSL are the main bodies for professional occupations.

English Test & Results

IELTS, PTE, TOEFL, or OET. Results typically in 2–4 weeks. Book early — testing centres have waiting periods.

Secure Job Offer & Sponsorship

Your employer must be an approved Standard Business Sponsor. Nomination lodgement takes 1–4 weeks on the employer's side.

SID Visa Application

Standard processing: 3–6 months. Priority processing (if available) can reduce this to 4–8 weeks for critical shortage roles.

Permanent Residency Pathway

After 2+ years on SID, eligible for ENS (subclass 186) permanent residency. Regional 491 holders can apply for subclass 191 after 3 years.

Frequently Asked Questions

What skills are most in demand in Australia in 2026?
According to the Jobs and Skills Australia 2025 Occupation Shortage List and LinkedIn's Jobs on the Rise 2026, the most in-demand skills in Australia are AI and Machine Learning, Registered Nursing, A-Grade Electricians, Cybersecurity, Software Engineering, Early Childhood Education, General Practice Medicine, Data Engineering, Clean Energy Engineering, and Aged Care. AI Engineering is the single fastest-growing job category in Australia per LinkedIn 2026.
What skills are on the Skills in Demand (SID) visa list in Australia?
The Skills in Demand visa (introduced 7 December 2024) is based on the Core Skills Occupation List (CSOL) — 456 occupations developed by Jobs and Skills Australia. Key CSOL occupations include Registered Nurses, Software Engineers, Electricians, Civil Engineers, GPs, Teachers, Aged Care Workers, and Data Engineers. The Core Skills Income Threshold is $76,515 (from July 2025). See immi.homeaffairs.gov.au for the full current list.
What is the Critical Skills Shortage List in Australia for 2025?
Australia's Occupation Shortage List (OSL), published by Jobs and Skills Australia in October 2025, identifies 29% of assessed occupations as in shortage. 139 occupations have been on the shortage list every year from 2021–2025. Healthcare, trades, and education have the highest shortage rates. 29 new occupations were added in 2025, including AI engineers and clinical psychologists. The full OSL is available at jobsandskills.gov.au.
What are the highest-paying in-demand skills in Australia?
The highest-paying in-demand skills in Australia 2025–26 are: Director of Artificial Intelligence (avg. $236,000), General Practitioner ($200K–$350K), Mining Electrician in WA ($175K–$200K), CISO/Cybersecurity Director ($200K–$350K), Finance Manager ($150K–$220K), and ML Engineer ($130K–$210K). Sydney and Melbourne pay 10–15% above national averages for technology roles. Source: Hays Salary Guide FY25–26 and LinkedIn Australian Tech Salary Guide 2025–26.
How can foreigners get jobs in Australia using in-demand skills?
Foreigners with an occupation on the CSOL can apply for the Skills in Demand visa via employer sponsorship. Key steps: (1) confirm your occupation is on the CSOL, (2) obtain a skills assessment from the relevant Australian body (e.g., AHPRA, Engineers Australia, AITSL), (3) secure a job offer from an approved Australian sponsor, (4) meet English requirements (IELTS 6.0+), and (5) meet the Core Skills Income Threshold of $76,515. Regional visa 491 offers bonus migration points for roles in regional Australia.
